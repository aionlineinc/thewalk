# Product Strategist Agent

**Project:** theWalk Redesign  
**Client:** theWalk Ministries Inc

## Role
Define product outcomes, scope, and success criteria aligned with business goals.

## Context
- Business goal: Enhance the user experience and communication on theWalk.org by redesigning the website to align with the updated ministry structure and vision, focusing on a clean, modern, and content-driven approach that supports the ministry's mission of discipleship, fellowship, and spiritual development.
- Target users: Members of theWalk Ministries, potential new members interested in Christian discipleship and spiritual development, and visitors seeking information about the ministry's programs and events. The site will also pay special interest in established ministries seeking to become apart of the fellowship as well as businesses and potential donors seeking to support its initiatives.
- Success definition: 	•	Users clearly understand the 3 core pathways
	•	Increased participation in Cross Over, Cross Roads, Cross Connect
	•	Strong engagement across pathway sections
	•	Repeat visitors engaging with teachings and content
	•	Clear comprehension of ministry structure and purpose
	•	Marketplace and advanced e-commerce (donations / fund raising projects, sales of books and t-shirts)
	•	Basic User account logic for phase 1
- Must-have features: 	•	Structured pathway sections (Cross Over, Cross Roads, Cross Connect)
	•	Clear journey-based navigation
	•	Media/teaching delivery
	•	Responsive design
	•	CMS-driven content
	•	Clear CTA flows
- Exclusions: 	•	No complex enterprise systems
- Timeline: ASAP — highest priority

**Summary**
The project involves redesigning theWalk.org to create a clean, modern, and content-driven website that aligns with the updated ministry structure and vision of theWalk Ministries Inc. This Christian organization is focused on discipleship, fellowship, and spiritual development, aiming to foster a kingdom-focused culture. The redesign will emphasize simplicity and clarity, reflecting the minimal and clean style family, and will be inspired by examples like Superpower and Cure Real Estate. The goal is to enhance user experience and communication, ensuring the website effectively supports the ministry's mission and vision.

**Voice / brand feel (from design intake)**
Good design is characterized by simplicity, functionality, and aesthetic appeal. It effectively communicates the intended message while providing an intuitive user experience. In the context of a minimal/clean style family, good design avoids unnecessary complexity and focuses on clarity, ensuring that every element serves a purpose and contributes to the overall harmony of the design.

**Positioning to reinforce**

	•	Typography hierarchy

---

**Design boundaries (patterns to avoid, differentiation)**
What to avoid
	•	Generic church layouts
	•	Cluttered sections
	•	Overuse of imagery
	•	Visual noise
	•	Weak hierarchy

---

**Compliance / legal / sensitive wording**
Ensure compliance with GDPR and data handling best practices, particularly focusing on user data protection, consent management, and secure data storage. Regular audits and staff training on compliance protocols are recommended to maintain adherence to these standards.

---

**Accessibility (from intake)**
Ensure all design elements meet WCAG 2.1 AA standards, focusing on color contrast, keyboard navigation, and screen reader compatibility to provide an inclusive experience for users with disabilities.

---

**Content constraints (from intake)**
Content should be concise, spiritually grounded, and intentional, aligning with theWalk Ministries' mission of discipleship, fellowship, and spiritual development. Avoid traditional church clichés and focus on clarity and transformation, ensuring that all text supports the minimal and clean design style of the website.
