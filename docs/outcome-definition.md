# Outcome Definition: theWalk Redesign

*Generated for theWalk Redesign. Use this to define "done" and align acceptance criteria and QA.*

## What done looks like
	•	Users clearly understand the 3 core pathways
	•	Increased participation in Cross Over, Cross Roads, Cross Connect
	•	Strong engagement across pathway sections
	•	Repeat visitors engaging with teachings and content
	•	Clear comprehension of ministry structure and purpose
	•	Marketplace and advanced e-commerce (donations / fund raising projects, sales of books and t-shirts)
	•	Basic User account logic for phase 1

## Target users and value
Members of theWalk Ministries, potential new members interested in Christian discipleship and spiritual development, and visitors seeking information about the ministry's programs and events. The site will also pay special interest in established ministries seeking to become apart of the fellowship as well as businesses and potential donors seeking to support its initiatives.

Enhance the user experience and communication on theWalk.org by redesigning the website to align with the updated ministry structure and vision, focusing on a clean, modern, and content-driven approach that supports the ministry's mission of discipleship, fellowship, and spiritual development.

## Must-have features (in scope)
	•	Structured pathway sections (Cross Over, Cross Roads, Cross Connect)
	•	Clear journey-based navigation
	•	Media/teaching delivery
	•	Responsive design
	•	CMS-driven content
	•	Clear CTA flows

## Out of scope
	•	No complex enterprise systems

## Design mode and quality bar
Design mode: clone  
What good design means for this project: Good design is characterized by simplicity, functionality, and aesthetic appeal. It effectively communicates the intended message while providing an intuitive user experience. In the context of a minimal/clean style family, good design avoids unnecessary complexity and focuses on clarity, ensuring that every element serves a purpose and contributes to the overall harmony of the design.
